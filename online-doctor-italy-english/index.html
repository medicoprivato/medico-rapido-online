Frequently asked questions

Do I need an Italian health card (tessera sanitaria)?

No. A private prescription (the Italian "ricetta bianca") from any licensed doctor is valid without a tessera sanitaria, without Italian residency, and without SSN registration. This is different from the public "ricetta rossa", which only SSN-affiliated doctors can issue.

Can I use your prescription to book a specialist visit through the public system (CUP)?

No, and it's important to be clear about this. Our private prescription can be used to buy medication at any pharmacy, or as documentation to show another doctor. It cannot replace the public "impegnativa", which only a doctor employed by or affiliated with the Italian public health system can issue.

If my partner and I both want to use the service, do we need separate subscriptions?

Yes. Each subscription is tied to one individual, with their own monthly request quota. If both of you want to use the service, you would each need your own €150/year subscription.

Is the written response really as valid as an in-person prescription?

Yes. Italy's 2022 National Telemedicine Guidelines explicitly recognize asynchronous written consultation as a valid way to deliver healthcare services, including prescriptions, when the clinical situation doesn't require a direct physical examination.
